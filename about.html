Shireen Abu Akleh (1971 - 2022) was a fearless Palestinian journalist who dedicated over 25 years to reporting the truth. Born in Jerusalem, she studied journalism at Yarmouk University and became one of the most trusted voices in Arab media. She joined Al Jazeera in 1997, covering wars, uprisings, and daily struggles of Palestinians. She believed in the power of storytelling, saying:

"I chose journalism to be close to the people. It is not easy to change reality, but at least I can bring their voices to the world."

Throughout her career, she risked her life to document the truth, standing on the frontlines despite the dangers. As she once said:

"The occupation does not scare me. I will continue until my voice reaches the world."

On May 11, 2022, while reporting in Jenin, she was shot and killed. Even in death, her legacy continues, proving that words can be stronger than bullets.
